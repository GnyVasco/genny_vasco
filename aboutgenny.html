About Me

Hi, my name is Genny Vasco. I have over two years of experience in Product Design, working remotely with both local and international teams, collaborating closely with developers, managers and graphic designers.

What drives my work is simple: creating technology that solves real problems without placing more barriers in front of people.

I've contributed as a Product Designer at 99 Interactions, MOZDEVZ, and as volunteer at GDSC and PyCon I contributed to help young Mozambican developers share their knowledge in Tech and help organizations improve their Digital products.

Through that work, I’ve gained not just professional experience, but also a clear sense of purpose: to collaborate with teams that value thoughtful, evidence-based software development and community impact. Especially now, in the age of AI, staying human-centered is essential.

That’s why I’m currently sharpening my skills through ALX Africa’s Data Science program, focusing on research methods, data analysis, and quantitative reasoning. I believe design decisions shouldn’t rely just on intuition but on real patterns and behaviors.

My process usually looks like this:

    Desk research, quantitative and qualitative research Brainstorming ideas, testing them, and iterating with my team Handoff and UX advocacy, ensuring the product stays clear, functional, and human-centered

I believe in getting ideas into people’s hands early, learning from what works, and improving through quick iterations. My strongest skills today include user research, prototyping, and agile product development. I work best in teams that care as much about the process as they do about the result.

If I had to sum up why I do what I do:

I believe technology in Africa doesn’t need to look like copy-paste versions of products from elsewhere. We can create our own. Thoughtful, well-researched, human-centered, made for African youth, and anyone else trying to do good work that helps people live better.
